An American
Education

Director Thai Bonapart explores a surprising chapter of her historically Black high school's history, when white students were bused in. The story challenges and twists the conventional narrative on busing, integration, and the South.

About the Film

West Charlotte High School has long been a cornerstone of its community. But one chapter of its history remains surprising, revelatory, and urgently relevant — a moment when integration was not only attempted, but truly lived. An American Education revisits that chapter, and asks what it means that it eventually unraveled.
